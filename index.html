Intro

A historic window has opened

Bitcoin’s Blockchain technology is at the advent of becoming critical infrastructure for the world. Smart contracts are finally unbounded on top of bitcoin. But mining does not reflect the “unbounded” nature of Satoshi’s Vision. The training wheels have been taken off, but mining is still constrained by the thinking of the past era. Our goal is to revitalize and re-innovate mining for this new era with a next generation on-chain mining pool and to fund it by conducting the first true crowdsale on top of Bitcoin.

Whether you're a miner, a smart-scrivener, a quantum information scientist or just a kitchen (al)chemist, you can now be part of minting a new era.

Info

Since 201* Bitcoin transactions which are smart contracts not only in their simplest most fundamnetal and prototypical form but always. But contracts have been limited on top of bitcoin by a bevy of arbitrary constraints. Among the the contract size limit, the opcode number limit, the AND & OR OPCODES being turned off. With the locks taken off and general contracting now possible on top of Bitcoin the relative the work that a miner does has changed in fundamental ways, A transaction could take significant work block of transactions The notion of the "work" that a miners does has been wrong-headed just like the locks on Bitcoin were wrong headed. Miners need a way for accounting for the whole work of the mining process now that the relative weight of the first component has changed.

For miners whose endeavours are rapidly commodotized over time the difference between accurately accounting for this work and not accurately accounting for this work can make a huge difference for a miner's bottom line But there's a probablem, how do you accurately account for the work that a miner does at any precicion? As previously discussed Bitcoin contracts can now finally be arbitrarily general and the work involved is not merely hashing but the running, validation, and processing of Bitcoin contracts. In order to measure the work that goes into a bitcoin transaction, and thereby the work that goes into a block of transactions and all the work of mining and Bitcoin altogether we need a way to measure the work that goes into general computation. There are many possible ways to approach this problem but we propose a novel cryptoeconomic method. Miners have an incentive to accurately report the work that they are doing if they are compensated for doing so. Storing and manipulating data so measuring general computation so we need a way to measure the work that goes into general computation. Manipulation of matter and manipulation of information

We propose a novel protocol consistent way to mine in order to In order to make One could presume that the work done by a miner is fully represented the the hash of a block header and the reward to a miner is the block reward. There is one way to measure work that is easy to validate and hard to fake: power. Computation, whether hashing, block validation, transaction validation requires energy block acceptance requires a dynamically self-adjusting cryptoeconomically enforced amount of work and miners must expend energy to do this work. Due to Bitcon's nature, we can measure this energy with cryptoeconomic guarantees and reward miners more accurately for their work. We propose a novel protocol consistent way to mine in order to achieve this.

Satoshi's ingenious protocol incentivize miners but do to the variance in order to smooth out this variance, lessen the burden of the risk, and ensure a regular stream of income miners invented mining pools. A group of miners collaborate by doing work to solve a block and distribute the winnings between But mining pools this necessitates trust miners must trust pool operaters and pool operators must trust miners else either party can be cheated out of their fair share of the reward.

Naturally various attacks on pools have developed over time. In an arms race various defensive strategies have developed to ensure fair payout to miners and pool. Another problem has emerged though mining pools are a point of centralizion in minning protocols. Pools act as gatekeepers determinning what transactions miners can work they are therefore a potential point of censorship in the network.

We propose a novel protocol consistent way to mine in order to rather than broadcasting shares to the network as p2pool miners does, a miners appends their hashes to a coinbase contract, thereby creating a mining oracle, from which they may withdraw tokenized shares according to a novel pool payout structure based on the energy necessary to do the computational work of mining.
